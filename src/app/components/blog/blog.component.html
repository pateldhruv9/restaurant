A salmon and avocado roll is a type of sushi that typically consists of bite-sized pieces of sushi rice and nori seaweed, filled with fresh, raw salmon and ripe, creamy avocado. The sushi roll may also include other ingredients, such as cucumber, scallions, or sesame seeds, and is often served with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger on the side. To make a salmon and avocado roll, sushi rice is cooked and seasoned with rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Nori sheets are then placed on a sushi mat, and a layer of rice is spread over the nori, leaving a small border around the edges. Thin slices of fresh salmon and ripe avocado are placed on top of the rice, and the roll is then tightly rolled up using the mat, so that the nori wraps around the filling. The roll is then sliced into bite-sized pieces, typically 6 to 8 pieces per roll, and served as a delicious and healthy appetizer or snack.
Pav bhaji

Pav bhaji is a popular Indian street food dish that originated in Mumbai. It consists of a spicy vegetable curry, typically made with potatoes, peas, tomatoes, onions, and a variety of spices, served with buttered and toasted pav buns.
Ingredients: 3 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
1 cup of peas
2 medium-sized onions, finely chopped
2 medium-sized tomatoes, finely chopped
2 tablespoons of butter
1 teaspoon of cumin seeds
1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste
2 teaspoons of pav bhaji masala
1 teaspoon of red chili powder
Salt to taste
2 tablespoons of coriander leaves, finely chopped
8 pav buns
Additional butter for toasting the buns
